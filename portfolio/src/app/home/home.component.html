About Me

Welcome, As you can gather from above - my name is Kristy Stallings, and I enjoy taking software from source control to end-users. I found my passion for software delivery after I took part in brutal manual software deployment. You know the type riddled with human mistakes and configuration issues. For some reason, the software only works on the development machine but not in any other environment. It becomes a game of spot the infrastructure difference. I never knew my Where's Waldo? skills would pay off so much in my career field. Is it weird that experience drove me to start a career in this field, Probably. Most sane people would run away at warp speed after that experience.

I got satisfaction after all the hard work to get everything up and running to see a fully functioning webpage finally, but I wanted to help improve the process. I quickly discovered all the issues in the manual deployment process I would like never to repeat. I looked through the list of the problems to work on, and it all came back to the same thing for improvements - automate it! I would love to pretend that some scripts were put in place, and everything just worked... but that wasn't the case. The first few attempts at an automated deployment were working through the errors. I spent an absurd amount of time reading deployment logs.

I went by the rule new errors equal PROGRESS! After numerous attempts, I saw the beautiful green checkmark of a successful deployment, and I was rewarded with working software. The team's efficiency with the automated pipeline was staggering. We went from manual deployments that could take days down to minutes for each deployment. I became hooked on automation. After that, I discovered the DevOps world, and I fell in love with the mindset for continuous improvement. Plus, the TOYS... I mean tools. Since then, I have been soaking in as much knowledge and experience as I can for the past three years. I have no plans to slow down.
